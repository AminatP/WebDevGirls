Effects

Now you know how climate change is caused, you might want to know how it affects you and why you should be worried about it. There are many effects of global warming, so let's dig depper and learn more about what dangers lie ahead.

Temperature

Rising temperature results in more droughts and heat waves. More and more areas are likely to be hit by extreme temperatures for longer time periods. It can result in changes in the body, and affect your mental and physical health. All the effects of climate change roots back to temperatures. The rising global heat causes all of these brutal effects.

Natural Disasters

Natural disaster are way more prone to occur. More strong and severe. Wildfires are more likely to spike, as the hot and dry temperatures as well as deforestation increase the risk of them. Hurricanes become stronger as they increase in number, duration and intensity. More water vapor is evaporated which fuels the hungry storms. Flooding will also occur with heavy precipitation. Natural disasters will only increase in the coming years.

Air Quality

Air quaity changes as more toxins are released into the air which makes it dangerous to breath. When the air is less healthy to breathe, it affects our health and wellbeing. Higher temperatures often result in an increase of allergens and air pollutants. This toxic air will be harmful to animals as well and will be fatal. Many species will be negatively affected and it will throw the entire animal kingdom out of balance. Poor air quality can also irritate the eyes, nose, and throat, aggravate respiratory conditions, and affect the heart. Long term affects include heart disease, lung cancer, and respiratory diseases. It can also damage your people's nerves, liver, brain, kidneys, and other organs

Water Effects

Water is a main victim of climate change. Essentialy, climate change is all about water. In fact when you observe all the effects of climate change, you will see that most of them have water as their source. Regarding evapration, warmer air holds more moisture this means that the air will absorb more water and results in drier conditions. It also can make the especially hot days unbearable, as it can be extremely humid, this overheats the body and blocks out sweat. Eventually the moisture packed air leads to precipitation and heavier storms. Some areas are expected to get drier and this results in more droughts. Sea life is being affected by these conditions and this affects the food chain. Glaciers and ice are melting and this contributes to rising sea levels and harms the fauna that live within the icy chambers. In addition, there is more rain rather than snow which means that the snow that slowly melts and adds to freshwater resources and replenishes drinking water supplies is fading away. This leads to drier conditions later in the year and water shortages. It also harms the wildlife that depend on it.
