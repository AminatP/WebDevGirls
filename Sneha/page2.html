Causes

You might be wondering how climate change is caused. Well, the causes of climate change are plentiful, so let's take a look at them.

The Greenhouse Effect

Climate change or global warming is largely caused by the greenhouse effect. The greenhouse effect is simple: We are entirely dependent on the energy from the Sun. Most of the light reaching Earth's atmosphere goes through the clouds and the air, eventually to the surface. This is where it is absorbed and radiated back to space in the form of heat. A huge percentage of this heat is sucked up by the greenhouse gases and bounces back to the surface.

Pollution

Pollution refers to anything that has a negative impact on the environment or organisms that live there. There are five major types of pollution. They include, air, water, soil, light and noise pollution. The main pollution that contribute to global warming is air pollution. This air pollution derives from houses, electricity, factories, cars and power plants that burn fossil fuels. Fossil fuels include: coal, oil, natural gas and gasoline. The result is heat trapping gases such as carbon dioxide and methane.

Littering

Littering and waste is another source of climate change. A lot of waste (other than sewage) ends up in dumpsters or landfills. This organice material decomposes and releaser toxins such as methane and carbon dioxide. This builds up and has and has a lasting impact on climate change. Litter outlives us all. It survives for long periods of time and directly affects climate change. For example plastic generates greenhouse gases even after it has been discarded and these emissions contribute to heating up the planet.

Deforestation

Deforestation is a major cause of climate change. Trees and other plants are crucial in the fight against climate change. But we lose our vital partner when we damage these ecosystems. Deforestation in its whole is the act of removing trees from a wide area of space. This may not seem like a big problem but trees are important because they take in carbon dioxide and release oxygen. By removing carbon dioxide, they are reducing the levels of this heat trapping gas. They also emit oxygen for us to breath. This balance is key, and we must protect it. Deforestation cuts down trees and ejects more and more carbon dioxide. Having this continue with no restrictions will be fatal.
